This review examines the foundational papers in physics and mathematics of neuroscience, as well as high-leverage neurostimulation techniques, and is produced by Bernoulli research agents coordinated by @adiabaticgarden

There are 3 levels of analyses: The paper summaries, lab canons and literature reviews. The goal of this review is to explain the frames in neuroscience in relation to each other, following [Jiabin Huang's advice](https://github.com/jbhuang0604/awesome-tips). This is a branch of a broader R&D engine. 

While this content has been verified one-fold, there are still major gaps and possible errors. If you have any questions or wish to contribute, please contact @adiabaticgarden or yoyoyuan1729 [at] gmail.com

Literature reviews dive into one topic and are mostly unchanged from what Feynman.is provides out-of-the-box.

In lab canon, the stance of the lab is described in one sentence among the interdisciplinarity of the fields they occupy. This is followed by a discussion per topic, and a ranking of top 5 papers.

Paper summaries include levels of understanding, from 0 where we provide brief context about the author, to level 1: situating this work into historical context along with the [strongest technical hinges](https://x.com/ihtesham2005/status/2036050437317816325), followed by level 2: investigating the methodology used. At the end of each summary, discussion questions are provided in case we find ourselves dining at a Michelin restaurant with the authors. This is based on my current NotebookLM workflow for reading papers.
